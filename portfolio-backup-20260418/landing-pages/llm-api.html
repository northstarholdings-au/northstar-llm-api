Compliance
Built for Australian
regulatory requirements

Three regulatory frameworks that US-hosted AI providers cannot satisfy. NorthStar addresses all three by design.

APRA CPS 230

Deadline: 1 July 2026

Financial services firms must ensure third-party AI contracts comply with operational risk standards. US-hosted AI creates unresolvable third-party risk. NorthStar keeps all processing in Australian jurisdiction.

My Health Records Act s77

In force now

Section 77 explicitly prohibits offshore processing of health records. Healthcare providers using US-hosted AI for clinical data may already be in breach. NorthStar is the compliant alternative.

Privacy Act Reforms

Deadline: December 2026

AI disclosure obligations take effect December 2026. NorthStar is compliant by default - no foreign jurisdiction, no retroactive patching required.

The US CLOUD Act problem

American cloud providers - including OpenAI, Microsoft Azure OpenAI, and AWS Bedrock - can be compelled by US law enforcement to hand over data stored or processed on their infrastructure. This applies regardless of where the data physically sits. NorthStar is an Australian entity operating Australian infrastructure. We cannot be compelled under US law. Ever.
